# Scoping — Define the Strategic Challenge

Transform a vague business challenge into a sharp, decision-oriented problem statement with clear scope.

## Process

### Step 1: Understand the Context
Ask the user:
- What is the strategic challenge or decision?
- Who is the client (company, industry, size)?
- What is the timeline for the decision?
- What has been tried already?

### Step 2: Sharpen the Problem
Apply the Precision Anchor framework:
- **Core question**: One sentence that captures the decision
- **Scope**: What is in/out of scope
- **Success criteria**: How will we know the answer is good
- **Constraints**: Budget, timeline, political, regulatory

### Step 3: Define the Deliverable
Confirm with the user:
- Format: Executive brief (4-6 pages) or comprehensive report (10-15 pages)?
- Audience: Who will read this? (CEO, board, team lead)
- Timeline: When is this needed?

### Step 4: Confirm
Present the problem statement and deliverable definition. Do not proceed until the user confirms.

## Output

A confirmed problem statement with:
- Core strategic question (1 sentence)
- Scope boundaries
- Success criteria
- Deliverable format and audience
