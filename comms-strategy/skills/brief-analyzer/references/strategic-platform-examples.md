# Strategic Platform Examples

Three worked examples of completed Strategic Platforms. Use these as quality references when building platforms from client briefs.

---

## Example 1: B2B Tech Brand — European Market Entry

**Context**: A US enterprise software company entering the French and German markets with a data analytics platform. Brief received from European marketing director.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                      STRATEGIC PLATFORM                             │
│ BRAND: DataVault Pro | CAMPAIGN TYPE: Market Entry                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ BUSINESS CHALLENGE                                                  │
│ DataVault Pro has 0% aided brand awareness in France and Germany    │
│ despite having 40% market share in North America. The European      │
│ market is dominated by SAP and Salesforce Analytics (combined 65%   │
│ share). Without European brand presence, the sales team cannot      │
│ convert even warm inbound leads — deals stall at "we've never       │
│ heard of you."                                                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ COMMUNICATIONS CHALLENGE                                            │
│ Shift European CIOs and Data Directors from "American software we   │
│ don't know" to "the serious challenger that our US counterparts     │
│ already rely on."                                                   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TARGET AUDIENCE                                                     │
│ Primary: CIOs and Data Directors at mid-large European enterprises  │
│ (500–5,000 employees), technology-progressive, benchmarking against │
│ US peers.                                                           │
│ Insight: European tech leaders don't want to be seen as behind the  │
│ US on data maturity — but they need to trust a vendor before giving │
│ them access to their most sensitive data.                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ INSIGHT                                                             │
│ European data leaders are under pressure to modernise faster, but   │
│ they hold the line on trust. They'll adopt American technology —    │
│ but only after European peers have validated it. They don't need    │
│ to be first. They need to be right.                                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ IDEA TERRITORY                                                      │
│ "The proof that travels" — European leaders adopt technology when   │
│ it's already proven elsewhere. Make US customer proof European-     │
│ relevant: stories of transformation, not feature lists.            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ MASTER MESSAGE                                                      │
│ DataVault Pro is already powering data decisions at 40% of Fortune  │
│ 500 companies — now it's built for European standards.             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ REASONS TO BELIEVE                                                  │
│ 1. 40% Fortune 500 market share (verified, published stat)         │
│ 2. Full GDPR compliance architecture (independently verified)      │
│ 3. First European client: [Flagship logo] live since Q1 2025       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TONALITY                                                            │
│ Authoritative but NOT arrogant. Proven but NOT boastful.           │
│ European-aware but NOT trying too hard.                            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ KPIs                                                                │
│ 1. Aided awareness: 0% → 15% among target CIOs in FR+DE (12 months)│
│ 2. Share of Voice in European data analytics press: 5% → 15%       │
│ 3. Inbound lead quality: sales-qualified leads from EU up 40%      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ BUDGET / TIMELINE                                                   │
│ Budget: €400K (PR, content, paid) | Timeline: 12 months           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ MANDATORY ELEMENTS                                                  │
│ GDPR compliance messaging mandatory in all materials.              │
│ No mention of US data sovereignty issues — legal sensitivity.      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Brief pathology identified**: This was a "feature brief" — original brief listed 24 product features. Reframed as an audience trust problem.

## Example 2: FMCG Brand — Relaunch Campaign

**Context**: French yoghurt brand relaunching after a 3-year absence. Brief from marketing manager, incomplete on KPIs and budget.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                      STRATEGIC PLATFORM                             │
│ BRAND: Fromagerie Bel — Le Rustique | CAMPAIGN TYPE: Relaunch      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ BUSINESS CHALLENGE                                                  │
│ After reformulation and 3 years off shelves, Le Rustique has        │
│ relisted in major French retailers but buyer memory is low —        │
│ distribution is conditional on sell-through in Q1.                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ COMMUNICATIONS CHALLENGE                                            │
│ Shift French households who've forgotten Le Rustique from           │
│ indifference to trial purchase within 8 weeks of relaunch.         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TARGET AUDIENCE                                                     │
│ Primary: Maîtresses de maison 35–55, regular buyers of             │
│ fromage de caractère, weekly supermarket shoppers.                 │
│ Insight: They want to feel clever about their food choices —       │
│ rediscovering a forgotten regional product is more appealing than  │
│ being sold to.                                                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ INSIGHT                                                             │
│ French consumers trust products with history more than products     │
│ with marketing. A return is a story; a new product is a pitch.     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ IDEA TERRITORY                                                      │
│ "It's back — and it's worth the wait." The product's absence is    │
│ reframed as a mark of care, not a commercial setback.              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ MASTER MESSAGE                                                      │
│ Le Rustique is back. Unchanged. Because some things shouldn't be   │
│ improved.                                                           │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ REASONS TO BELIEVE                                                  │
│ 1. Same farmhouse recipe (terroir guarantee)                       │
│ 2. Same producer relationships (provenance story)                  │
│ 3. Consumers who remembered it are already talking — [UGC quotes] │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TONALITY                                                            │
│ Warm but NOT sentimental. Confident but NOT defensive.             │
│ French but NOT clichéd.                                            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ KPIs [TBC — needs client confirmation]                              │
│ 1. Trial purchase intent among target audience: >25% exposed       │
│ 2. Earned media coverage: 5+ food press placements in weeks 1–3   │
│ 3. Social UGC: 200+ organic posts in first 4 weeks                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ BUDGET / TIMELINE                                                   │
│ Budget: [TBC — brief silent, hypothesis: €150–200K]                │
│ Timeline: 8-week campaign, launch week TBC                         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ MANDATORY ELEMENTS                                                  │
│ Must feature Normandy origin prominently. No price promotion.      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Brief pathology identified**: "Awareness brief" with no commercial conversion link. Reframed as a trial-purchase brief. KPIs flagged as [TBC] — needs client sign-off.

## Example 3: Corporate / Institutional — CSR Communication

**Context**: Large French energy company communicating their decarbonisation roadmap. Brief from head of corporate communications.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                      STRATEGIC PLATFORM                             │
│ BRAND: [Energy Co.] | CAMPAIGN TYPE: Corporate Comms / CSR        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ BUSINESS CHALLENGE                                                  │
│ Company has committed to net-zero by 2040. Activist groups,        │
│ institutional investors, and regulators are all asking for proof   │
│ of credibility — the roadmap exists but isn't reaching the right   │
│ audiences with enough specificity to change the conversation.      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ COMMUNICATIONS CHALLENGE                                            │
│ Shift institutional stakeholders (investors, regulators, NGOs)     │
│ from sceptical observers to cautious believers by making the       │
│ decarbonisation commitment specific, evidenced, and accountable.   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TARGET AUDIENCE                                                     │
│ Primary: ESG-mandate institutional investors + French government   │
│ energy regulators.                                                 │
│ Secondary: Financial press + general public (as legitimacy layer). │
│ Insight: Investors don't trust energy company climate pledges —    │
│ they've heard them before. Specificity and third-party validation  │
│ are the only credibility levers that work.                         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ INSIGHT                                                             │
│ "Net zero by 2040" is believed by no one until it comes with a     │
│ named milestone, a measurable intermediate target, and a penalty   │
│ for missing it. Ambition without accountability is greenwashing.   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ IDEA TERRITORY                                                      │
│ "Accountability in plain sight" — make every commitment verifiable │
│ in real-time. Radical transparency as the differentiator in a      │
│ category where opacity is the norm.                                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ MASTER MESSAGE                                                      │
│ Every milestone is public. Every miss will be explained.           │
│ That's the only promise worth making.                              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ REASONS TO BELIEVE                                                  │
│ 1. Public milestone tracker (live dashboard, not PDF roadmap)      │
│ 2. Third-party auditor: [Named organisation] certifies annually    │
│ 3. Executive remuneration: 30% tied to carbon KPIs                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TONALITY                                                            │
│ Direct but NOT defensive. Transparent but NOT self-congratulatory. │
│ Serious but NOT technocratic.                                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ KPIs                                                                │
│ 1. ESG analyst ratings: target upgrade from 2 agencies by year-end │
│ 2. Positive institutional investor coverage: 10+ mentions in       │
│    specialist ESG press in 6 months                                │
│ 3. Regulator engagement: 2+ formal acknowledgements of roadmap     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ BUDGET / TIMELINE                                                   │
│ Budget: €250K (thought leadership, events, press)                  │
│ Timeline: 18-month sustained programme                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Brief pathology identified**: "Vanity brief" — original brief led with deliverables (white paper, LinkedIn series, event) before defining the strategic problem. Reversed to strategy-first.
