# KPI Benchmarks and Measurement Reference

Detailed benchmarks, industry standards, and measurement methodology references for communications KPIs. Loaded on demand when defining a measurement framework.

---

## Level 1: Activity KPIs — Benchmarks

### Press / Earned Media

| KPI | Good | Strong | Exceptional | Notes |
|-----|------|--------|-------------|-------|
| Press hits (national) | 3–5 | 6–15 | 15+ | Per campaign launch |
| Press hits (trade) | 5–10 | 10–25 | 25+ | Per month (sustained programme) |
| Tier 1 hit rate | >20% | >35% | >50% | % of all hits in top-tier press |
| Key message penetration | >40% | >60% | >75% | % of articles carrying ≥1 KM |
| Positive sentiment | >70% | >80% | >90% | % of coverage with positive tone |
| Domain authority of coverage | DA 30+ | DA 50+ | DA 70+ | Average authority of placing sites |

### Share of Voice Benchmarks

| SOV position | Market share implication | Strategic action |
|-------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| SOV > Market share by 10%+ | Momentum — brand is growing faster than market | Sustain investment |
| SOV = Market share (±5%) | Equilibrium — brand holding position | Maintain or invest ahead of growth |
| SOV < Market share by 10%+ | Erosion — competitors are outvoicing the brand | Increase investment or reposition |
| SOV > 50% | Category leader — must defend | Focus on differentiation over volume |

**The Excess Share of Voice (ESOV) rule**: For every 10 points of ESOV (SOV minus market share), a brand can expect approximately 0.5% market share growth per year (IPA/Binet & Field). ESOV must be sustained for at least 18 months for market share effect to materialize.

## Level 2: Audience Perception KPIs

### Brand Tracking Metrics

| Metric | Definition | How to measure | Benchmark rate of change |
|--------|-----------|---------------|--------------------------|
| **Unaided brand awareness** | % who spontaneously name the brand when asked about category | Quant survey | +2–5pp per sustained campaign year |
| **Aided brand awareness** | % who recognise brand when prompted | Quant survey | +5–10pp per campaign |
| **Brand consideration** | % who would consider the brand at next purchase | Quant survey | Lags awareness by 6–12 months |
| **Brand preference** | % who prefer the brand vs. alternatives | Quant survey | Lags consideration by 3–6 months |
| **Brand recommendation (NPS)** | Net Promoter Score | Regular pulse survey | Varies; B2B: 40+ good, B2C: 30+ good |

**Survey sample guidance:**
- Minimum sample for statistical significance: 200 per target segment
- Wave frequency: Pre-campaign, mid-campaign (for 6+ month campaigns), post-campaign
- Control group: Where possible, compare a region/segment not exposed to the campaign

### Sentiment Measurement

Three-tier sentiment classification (avoid binary positive/negative):

| Tier | Label | Definition | Action |
|------|-------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Strongly positive | Advocates, recommends, defends | Amplify |
| 2 | Positive | Favourable coverage, no criticism | Sustain |
| 3 | Neutral | Factual, no clear valence | Monitor |
| 4 | Mixed | Positive with material criticism | Respond |
| 5 | Negative | Predominantly critical | Escalate |
| 6 | Crisis-level | Reputational threat | Immediate action |

## Level 3: Behaviour KPIs

### Digital Attribution Methods

| Method | Accuracy | Implementation | Best for |
|--------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **UTM tracking** | High (direct traffic) | URL parameters | Paid, owned, earned (with branded links) |
| **Promo codes** | High (direct) | Unique codes per channel | Influencer, radio, print |
| **Brand search lift** | Medium (proxy) | Google Trends + Search Console | Awareness campaign measurement |
| **Direct traffic uplift** | Medium | GA baseline comparison | Campaign period vs. control |
| **Multi-touch attribution** | Medium | Analytics platform (GA4) | Full funnel |
| **Marketing Mix Modelling** | High (retrospective) | Econometric model | Long-term ROI, multi-channel |

**Brand search as a proxy metric**: During campaigns, an increase in branded search volume (Google Search Console or Google Trends) is a reliable proxy for campaign-driven awareness and intent. Segment by geography for regional campaigns.

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Action | Typical conversion rate | Depends on |
|--------|------------------------|------------|
| Landing page → email sign-up | 2–8% | Offer quality, page design |
| Landing page → purchase | 1–4% | Price point, trust level |
| Video view → website visit | 0.5–2% | CTA strength, relevance |
| Press coverage → direct traffic | 0.1–1% | Authority of placing media |
| Influencer post → click | 0.5–3% | Influencer trust, product relevance |

## Level 4: Business Impact Attribution

### Attribution Frameworks

**Contribution claiming** (recommended for comms): Rather than claiming causal attribution (which requires econometric modelling), comms teams can claim:

> "During the campaign period, [metric] increased by [X%] vs. the prior comparable period, in a context where comms activity was [X] times higher than baseline."

This is correlation-based claiming, which is defensible and transparent.

**Incremental lift testing**: For digital campaigns, A/B test between exposed and unexposed audiences (geographic or demographic holdout) to measure true incremental effect.

### Long-Term Brand Value Metrics

Beyond campaign KPIs, sustainable comms measurement includes:

| Metric | What it tracks | How to measure |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Share of voice trend (12M) | Brand's position in category conversation over time | Monthly media monitoring |
| Brand equity score | Combined awareness + consideration + preference + advocacy | Quarterly brand tracker |
| Earned media value | Total PR reach × audience quality index | Agency calculation |
| Thought leadership index | Share of expert/commentary placements vs. news mentions | Media audit |
| Employee sentiment | Internal brand advocacy | Quarterly internal survey (eNPS) |

## Reporting Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-pattern | Why it's wrong | Replace with |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|
| AVE (Advertising Value Equivalent) | Uses ad rate as proxy for editorial value — discredited since 2010 (Barcelona Principles) | Audience reach × sentiment × message penetration |
| Impressions as success | Potential exposure ≠ actual attention | Add time-spent and engagement as qualifiers |
| Vanity metrics alone | Followers, likes don't track to business outcomes | Anchor every metric to a funnel level |
| SOV without context | SOV going up means nothing if the category is shrinking | Always show SOV vs. market share trend |
| Counting all coverage equally | One BBC article ≠ one local blog | Use tiered weighting (Tier 1 / 2 / 3) |

## The Barcelona Principles (Updated 2020)

The industry-standard framework for PR measurement. Seven principles:

1. Setting goals is an absolute prerequisite for communications planning and measurement
2. Measuring communication outcomes is recommended versus only measuring outputs
3. The effect on organisational performance can and should be measured where possible
4. Measurement and evaluation require both qualitative and quantitative methods
5. AVEs are not the value of communications
6. Social media can and should be measured consistently with other media channels
7. Measurement and evaluation should be transparent, consistent, and valid
