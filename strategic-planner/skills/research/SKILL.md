# Research — Parallel Investigation

Deploy two independent research agents from complementary angles, then validate for consistency and source quality.

## Process

### Step 1: Brief
Create a research brief from the problem statement. Include:
- Core question to answer
- Key dimensions to investigate
- Known data sources
- Specific hypotheses to test

### Step 2: Parallel Research
Dispatch two agents simultaneously:

**researcher-a** — Direct angle:
- Market size, growth, trends
- Financial benchmarks
- Industry structure analysis
- Regulatory environment

**researcher-b** — Complementary angle:
- Competitive dynamics and positioning
- Case studies (successes and failures)
- Contrarian evidence
- Customer/market sentiment

### Step 3: Validation
Dispatch **validator** to:
- Cross-check findings for consistency
- Score each source (CS-1 to CS-4)
- Identify contradictions and gaps
- Flag unsourced claims

### Step 4: Present
Present the validated research package to the user:
- Executive summary (5-10 key findings)
- Source registry with credibility scores
- Identified gaps and uncertainties

**Checkpoint**: User reviews and approves before proceeding.

## Source Credibility Scale

| Score | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| CS-1 | Primary source, verified | Government data, company filings |
| CS-2 | Reputable secondary | Major research firm, quality press |
| CS-3 | Single source, unverified | Vendor claim, blog post |
| CS-4 | Low reliability | Anonymous, outdated |

Only CS-1 and CS-2 data should anchor key conclusions. CS-3 can support but not lead.
